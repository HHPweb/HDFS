Alternative Routes to FACS Licensure

If you already have a bachelor's degree and wish to teach in classrooms requiring a FACS license you, can find information on the Office of Alternative Licensure East Carolina University's webpage. Additional information and sample plans of study are available for each alternate route described below. For information about applying for admission, click Resources and Forms on the Alternative Licensure webpage.

The Licensure Only option is for students who already have a four year college degree but no teaching license. If you have a bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited institution and wish to obtain a FACS license, you may do so through Licensure Only. The Office of Alternative Licensure will evaluate individual transcripts and students will receive a Plan of Study. In your letter requesting a Licensure Only Plan of Study, indicate your desired area of licensure as Family and Consumer Sciences.

The Lateral Entry option allows an individual to teach while completing their coursework. Lateral entry teachers are individuals employed in a North Carolina school system that hold at least a bachelor's degree from a regionally-accredited institution in the subject area they are employed to teach. Graduates of approved education programs who are not recommended for licensure by their college or university are not eligible for a lateral entry license. Lateral entry teachers who affiliate with ECU are advised through the Office of Alternative Licensure in the Office of Teacher Education. Information can be found at the Lateral Entry webpage.

Licensure requirements can be met through a traditional face-to-face classroom environment or via distance education.
